Bone Marrow Transplant (BMT) better known as haematopoietic stem cell transplant(HSCT) as bone marrow cells are transplanted



Replacement of diseased or defective marrow with marrow from a healthy donor

Medicines and in certain situations radiotherapy as well is given to get rid of diseased marrow and bone marrow cells from healthy donor is given to replace it.


IN autologous bone marrow transplant, high dose chemotherapy is given to kill cancer cells and child own marrow cells are given back

Stem Cell Transplant: Also referred to as Bone Marrow Transplant, this procedure is implemented to replace the unhealthy blood-forming cells with healthy ones. Stem-cell treatment gives the doctors, chance to give huge doses of Chemotherapy or Radiation Therapy to boost the chance of removing Blood Cancer and other forms of Cancer. Allotransplants: This is the transplantation of tissues, cells, or organs to a recipient from a genetically non-identical donor and the transplant is known as an Allogeneic Transplant, Allograft Transplant, or Homograft. Allotransplantation is very commonly used in the cure of end-stage Liver, Lung, Kidney and Heart diseases. Paediatric Chemotherapy: Chemotherapy is one of the most matured forms of Cancer treatment and thus can safely be used for the treatment of Cancer in children. The number of drugs used varies as per the type of Leukaemia or Cancer the child is suffering from. This may be used alone or with other treatments like surgery or Radiation. It interferes into the Cancer cell’s ability to grow or multiply. Palliative Bio-chemotherapy: This is Chemotherapy administered in the non-curative setting to lower the burden of Cancer-related symptoms, enhance the quality of life, and also, to improve survival chances. This has shown very positive results for patients suffering from high risk Cancers. Concurrent Bio-Radiotherapy: Dr. Raina is especially efficient in implementation of this therapy in the treatment of Head and Neck Cancers. This treatment technique has been observed to enhance the overall condition of the patients. Systemic chemotherapy: Usually, Chemotherapy is given Systemically, where the Cancer drugs are pass in the body via the mouth, veins, or muscles and reach the bloodstream. This way the medicine reaches the Cancerous cells. At the same time, the medicine also reaches the healthy cells because of which there can be side-effects.

What are the types of Bone Marrow Transplant?

  • Autologous Bone Marrow Transplant - Marrow cells are collected from the patient’s own body and transfused back after high dose chemotherapy.
  • Allogenic Bone Marrow Transplant (Matched Sibling Donor) – Replacement of diseased or defective marrow with marrow from a genetically (HLA) matched sibling donor
  • Allogenic Bone Marrow Transplant (Matched Unrelated Donor) – Replacement of diseased or defective marrow with marrow from a genetically (HLA) matched donor obtained from bone marrow registries in India and abroad
  • Haplo-Identical Bone Marrow Transplant - Replacement of diseased or defective marrow with marrow from the parent.
  • Umbilical cord Bone Marrow Transplant - Replacement of diseased or defective marrow with marrow from a genetically (HLA) matched umbilical cord donor obtained from umbilical cord registries in India and abroad.
